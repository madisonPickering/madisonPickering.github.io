  • [2022] Implementation of Outpatient Infectious Diseases E-Consults at a Safety Net Healthcare System

    Project focused on the outcomes and characteristics of patients who were referred for E-consults.   • [2020] NetworkSIR And EnvironmentalSIR: Two Simple Distributed Mechanisms For Modeling Epidemics

    Madison Pickering. [Link] [Code Repository Information]

    This is my Master's thesis, whom I was advised by Dr. Subbarayan Venkatesan at UT Dallas for. It provides an in-depth discussion of assumptions made by various disease modeling paradigms, extends the SIR model to adapt it to a distributed system (NetworkSIR), and then extends NetworkSIR in order to model indirect disease spread through the environment (EnvironmentalSIR). As far as I know, it is the first network-based model that captures environment-to-human disease spread.

    There is unfortunately some errata associated with this document: the equation on bullet point 2, page 27 is incorrect, which has slightly thrown off some of the numbers and graphs in the results and discussion. The more recent paper on NetworkSIR has fixed this, and recollected all the data discussed in this thesis; please refer to it for more accurate results.
