Publications and Submitted Papers

  • [2021] Characteristics and Outcomes of an Outpatient ID E-consult Program at a County Safety Net Healthcare System

    Madison Granger, Richard Medford, Madison Pickering, Helen King. (Author list tentative) [Abstract coming soon!]

    Project focused on the outcomes and characteristics of patients who were referred for E-consults. ML models were also trained with patient information gathered a priori to determine if an E-consult would require a face to face meeting. In other words, the model was trained to determine if a face to face referral to a specialist would likely be required given the information known a priori about the patient.

  • [2021] Outcomes of Patients Contracting COVID-19 after receiving a SARS-CoV-2 Vaccination: A Nationwide Retrospective Study

    Sameh Saleh, Madison Pickering, Dimpy Shah, Christoph Lehmann, Richard Medford. [Abstract coming soon!]

    Project studied the outcomes of individuals who contracted COVID-19 after recieving one or more vaccinations, as opposed to individuals who did not recieve a vaccine. The major competitive advantage this study had was its generalizability and large sample size—similar studies could often only work with the data available at a single hospital, but the data set we used was taken over millions of patients at healthcare institutions all over the United States.

  • [2020] NetworkSIR And EnvironmentalSIR: Two Simple Distributed Mechanisms For Modeling Epidemics

    Madison Pickering. [Link] [Code Repository Information]

    This is my Master's thesis, whom I was advised by Dr. Subbarayan Venkatesan at UT Dallas for. It provides an in-depth discussion of assumptions made by various disease modeling paradigms, extends the SIR model to adapt it to a distributed system (NetworkSIR), and then extends NetworkSIR in order to model indirect disease spread through the environment (EnvironmentalSIR). As far as I know, it is the first network-based model that captures environment-to-human disease spread.

    There is unfortunately some errata associated with this document: the equation on bullet point 2, page 27 is incorrect, which has slightly thrown off some of the numbers and graphs in the results and discussion. The more recent paper on NetworkSIR has fixed this, and recollected all the data discussed in this thesis; please refer to it for more accurate results.
