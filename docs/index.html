Bio

I am currently a second-year Ph.D student in Computer Science at the University of Chicago. I am a member of UChicago's SUPERGroup, led by Dr. Blase Ur. Most of my work right now involves working with large transformer-based language models like those in the GPT family, of which ChatGPT is probably the most famous. I am particularly interested in what it means for a large language model to have a "bug", as well as topics surrounding transformer's security applications.


My research interests generally lie at the union of security, machine learning, and HCI. I also like parallelism and distributed systems, which I pursued more directly while I was a student at UT Dallas.

I worked predominantly with Dr. Richard Medford, Dr. Christoph Lehmann, and Dr. Sameh Saleh of UTSouthwestern's Clinical Informatics Center prior to starting my Ph.D. My work while at UTSouthwestern ultimately culminated in appointments as Trainee Member and POI of the center.

I enjoy teaching in positions that require me to work directly with students, and do so at least once a year. If I am your TA for a class, please don't tweet at me. But, do come to office hours.

I am also proudly Texan (born n' raised), biracial, bisexual, and nonbinary. Please feel free to refer to me with any pronouns you like.
