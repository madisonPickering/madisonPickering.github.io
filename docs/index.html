Bio

I am a fifth-year Computer Science PhD student at the University of Chicago. I am advised by Blase Ur and am a member of UChicago's SUPERGroup. My research investigates how, if, or when LLMs can be used as an interface for meeting end-user privacy and security needs. I have explored how LLMs may exhibit undesirable behavior when completing everyday, agentic tasks like booking a flight; how they might be used to determine if a user's password is weak so a user can change it; and for facilitating normative privacy policy annotation so that end-users can intuitively understand a privacy policy's impact.

I am also proudly Texan (born n' raised), biracial, bisexual, and nonbinary (they/them). I powerlift, play music, and enjoy very nerdy tabletop games in my free time.
